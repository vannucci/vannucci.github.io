I am a 36 year old web developer who has approximately one year under by belt. In my past life I was a high school physics teacher and I taught for seven years in various suburban NJ high schools. I came from a family tradition of education, having two parents with advanced degrees, many uncles and aunts with them, and both sibings with degrees. So needless to say, being in schools was a part of who I was. But I felt unfulfilled as a teacher, and it seemed like my efforts would go unrewarded over the coming years. What I mean is that it always felt like no matter how hard I worked to teach my students or be a good professional, I always got the same results from them.

In fact this above feeling is something I've often felt. That putting in extra effort doesn't matter, I'm just spinning my wheels over doing more since my outcomes are the same. Is this my anxiety, my stress, my ADHD or even just my overall mentality towards life? Why put in more effort since you'll only just get the same results?

Wow that's a big connection for me right there. It really describes a lot of how I've done many things like my own relationships, physical fitness, school and my career.
