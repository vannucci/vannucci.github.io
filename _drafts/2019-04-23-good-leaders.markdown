See here's the thing, and I think if or when you make it to some sort of leadership role I think you need to remember this

Mark isn't a bad manager because he doesn't take things into consideration
Or doesn't understand why the way we do things is bad
He's a bad manager because he doesn't listen to any of his subordinates when they clearly spell out WHY this isn't good
Think of the classic, Lieutenant and Sergeant role in movies
The LT gives orders, and the SGT is the XO for those orders
In public, they are a united front.
But in private the SGT has enough social capital to say to the LT "Hey this thing you're doing is wrong/bad and here's why and what you should do"
Mark could have had that sort of relationship with you
But what he has instead is the mentality of an LT whose company is lethargic, disinterested, and ultimately gets them all killed by doing something stupid which nobody could stop him OR they all just do their own thing (which we do)
If (or when) you find yourself in a leadership role, you need someone who is publicly your supporter and privately your equal. Listen to your own intuition and let that person call you out when you do stupid shit
A leader cannot possibly know everything that's right. That's not even remotely possible
But good leaders take the smartest and most capable people and tell them, "This is yours to do and it needs to get done. Take responsibility for this." When the fuck up, they have a trusted confidant that can and does call them out, always in private, and the leader _listens_
I know you're working hard right now and sorry for the wall of text, but I think you'd be a good leader or at least a competent one.
