I want to show you how I wrote this post, using a technique I read about in the hackernews comments section.

This is how my post began:

-----
## Working by Comments

1. Ideation of a good post (call to adventure)
1. Research (supernatural aid)
1. Overthinking and Just Start Writing (threshold guardian)
1. Helper and Mentor (write by comments)
1. Anxiety, Loss of emotional control, Procrastination (Challenges and temptation)
1. Stop working and never come back (ABYSS, DEATH AND REBIRTH)
1. Doing one shitty comment section at a time (transformation)
1. Seeing my procrastination for what it is, fear (atonement)
1. Reading the whole thing, connecting it (atonement)
1. Posting (return)

-----

These ten bullet points took me a little longer than normal, but I wanted to craft my writing in the form of a [Hero's Journey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hero%27s_journey). In the past I would look at a self-imposed writing assignment and just get stuck at step 5 and never come back. But when I read the idea of 'writing by comments' I realized that this was an excellent technique for fooling my brain.

Write what you _want_ something to do, and then gradually fill it in so that it _does_.
